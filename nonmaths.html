Philosophy & writing

As I said, I love philosophy, more specifically I am interested in existentialism and absurdism (both worthy solutions of nihilism!). I’m really passionate about writing and hope to publish more on my personal blog. I am quite a reflective person (or so I've been told!) and I need a medium to let all my reflections outwards.

Books that influence me

I have quite an interesting relationship with literature and books in general. Some books I read shaped who I am and, for the past year, some books literally helped me recover my language cognition skills. I will outline those books that, if you were to read them, you'd understand a bit better why I am the way I am.

  • The Stranger by Albert Camus
  • The Gay Science by Friedrich Nietzsche
  • Iona by Marin Sorescu
  • Kafka on the Shore by Haruki Murakami
  • A Mathematician's Lament by Paul Lockhart
  • Love and Math: The Heart of Hidden Reality by Edward Frenkel
  • How I Wish I’d Taught Maths by Craig Barton

Other hobbies

Outside my academic and philosophical interests, I enjoy volunteering and of course, writing. I see these activities as part of the same curiosity that drives my work in mathematics and education.

Last time when I volunteered, I helped out at the Manchester Literature Festival. It was an amazing experience where I got to hear spoken word on reggae beats, meet Marcus du Sautoy, and hear the poems of Seamus Heaney.
