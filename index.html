About me

I am Sonia, a Mathematics student at the University of Manchester. My main interests are in abstract algebra (especially non-commutative algebra) and mathematics education.

Around these, I have collected a number of roles in the department and across the university: Library Student Team member, Student Rep Coordinator, PASS (Peer-assisted Study Sessions) Coordinator, MathSoc committee member, and general helper with departmental initiatives. A common thread is that I like working behind the scenes to make things run more smoothly for other students.

Outside of mathematics, I am very attached to continental philosophy, reading, and writing, and I am slowly finding ways to let these interests talk to each other. I am originally from Romania, and Manchester has become an intellectual home for me.

The picture above was taken when I received 3rd place in the University of Manchester “Volunteer of the Year Award” for work supporting other students.

Maths & academic projects

Maths stuff

My maths interests, as I said, extend in two dimensions: abstract algebra and maths education. I am of the firm conviction that the scientific community is really bad at communicating and this is why maths has such a bad name. I will probably write a reflective piece on this but for the time being, I recommend you read Paul Lockhart's A Mathematician's Lament.

Books that influence me

I have quite an interesting relationship with literature and books in general. Some books I read shaped who I am and, for the past year, some books literally helped me recover my language cognition skills. I will outline those books that, if you were to read them, you'd understand a bit better why I am the way I am.

  • The Stranger by Albert Camus
  • The Gay Science by Friedrich Nietzsche
  • Iona by Marin Sorescu
  • Kafka on the Shore by Haruki Murakami
  • A Mathematician's Lament by Paul Lockhart
  • Love and Math: The Heart of Hidden Reality by Edward Frenkel
  • How I Wish I’d Taught Maths by Craig Barton
