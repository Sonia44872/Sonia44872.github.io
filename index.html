About Me

Hello, I am Sonia (please call me Sonia, not Sarah!) Firstly, I am currently a Mathematics student at the University of Manchester. My interests lie in Abstract Algebra (in particular, non-commutative algebra!) and Maths Education. Secondly, I have a lot of roles at the University: Library Student Team member, Student Rep Coordinator, Peer-assissted Study Sessions Coordinator, MathSoc committee member, and overall helper with departmental initiatives (this was a mouthful). Thirdly, I am passionate about continental philosophy, reading, and writing. I hope that one day I will find a way to bridge these hobbies together. Until then, I will keep on exploring the world and myself!

What do I do?

You will find a common denominator amongst my roles: I like working behind the scenes.

Library Student Team Member

I got into the role in September 2024. More generally, the role implies facilitating and helping refresh the study-skills workshops delivered by the library to UoM students. More specifically, I help refresh and QA the workshops to make sure they are relevant, representative and accessible to students. I was also involved in a bunch of other projects, the highlight being helping design and deliver a poster presentation at the Institute of Teaching and Learning conference about alternative assessments in HE.

Student Rep Coordinator

I am passionate to drive change at this University. My role involves making the student rep system more visible and overseeing that the feedback loops are done properly within the student body.

PASS Coordinator

As a PASS Coordinator, I design the weekly teaching materials based on activities I have researched in my summer maths education project and train PASS Leaders (the ones delivering them) on how to better facilitate the activities (e.g. Self- and peer-feedback, Self-explanation, Distributed learning, etc.)

MathSoc Committee Member

In MathSoc I make sure that the "academic" activities run smoothly, including taking the lead for two years on organising a UK-wide maths competition.

Manchester Interdisciplinary Mathematics Undergraduate Conference Organiser

This role speaks to my values of having an interdisciplinary, communal approach to maths.

Fellowship of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA) — Pending
